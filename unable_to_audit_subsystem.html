How an audit gets abandoned, the books get repaired,
and the work gets replaced

The problem

An auditor opens an encounter and cannot audit it

  • The chart does not match the encounter, or the encounter itself can never be evaluated
  • The audit can never complete, so it must leave every queue for good
  • The chart can no longer be trusted, so it must never be selected again
  • The coder's progress already counted this audit the moment it was created
  • The program still owes the client a real audit in its place
1 = 1
Every program audit carries exactly one progress credit, earned at creation

Why this shape

All or nothing, then tell the world

  • The stamp, the discards, the chart, and the decrement commit together or not at all
  • Events fire only after commit, so listeners never observe uncommitted state
  • Chart matching uses .sole: an ambiguous or incomplete composite deactivates nothing, logged and moved on
  • Re-running the form on an abandoned audit returns true without re-stamping or re-emitting

04

The books

One decrement, one increment, net zero

debit

The decrement

  • CreditReversal runs inside the abandon transaction, so it applies exactly once or not at all
  • The bucket is the one the audit was credited to: audit_month when present, its creation month otherwise
  • decrement_for! takes the same row lock as reserve_slot!, so a concurrent reservation cannot interleave
  • Returns :decremented, :no_bucket (expected legacy), or :at_zero (accounting anomaly, logged at warn)
  • A DB CHECK keeps credits_count >= 0 no matter what
credit

The increment

  • The replacement reserves its credit at creation through the same atomic reserve_slot! the trigger batch uses, keyed to the abandoned audit's month
  • Whoever reserves first wins the freed slot, so a concurrent trigger batch can never double-fill it
  • ProgressHandlerJob skips crediting replacements, exactly like trigger audits, but still runs the graduation check
  • A failed create (cycle settled, chart claimed) releases the reservation, so no phantom credit survives
  • Freed slot minus one, replacement plus one: the coder's target stays whole

Programs & graduation

The graduated coder is the common case

  • Coders graduate the moment their final audit is created, not when it is completed
  • Most audits generate early in the month, so by audit time the coder is often already active in the next program
  • The replacement is created back in the graduated program, from a chart coded in that window, so graduation stays based on real audits
  • Auditors work oldest-first, so the backfill surfaces right away
10 / 10
The graduated program's audit count stays whole, every credit backed by a real evaluation
monthly cycles

Backfill the month, never the released

  • Replacements are stamped with the abandoned audit's month and cycle, so attribution lands in the right period
  • A settled cycle (released or externally rejected) denies the replacement, and the cycle row is re-locked at create time to re-check
  • Anything already exported to Platform is immutable, cycles are built from completed evaluations and the abandoned one is discarded
initial cycles

The replacement is the fast path

  • An initial cycle reaches review only through target_met?, which counts completed evaluations
  • Abandon one of ten and the backfill refills the tenth slot within seconds, keeping the cycle on the normal road to review
  • When no chart exists to backfill, the cycle closes short instead of waiting forever, next slide

When no chart exists

A cycle that can't fill closes short

  • closed? means the cycle can no longer receive evaluations: an elapsed period for monthly, a completed membership for initial, and no audit still open, counting every audit of the coder in the program, attached to the cycle or not
  • The nightly PeriodRolloverJob sweeps unapproved cycles past their intake window and hands closed ones to the Orchestrator
  • A short cycle faces the same gates as a full one: below threshold holds for external review, at or above auto-releases
  • Settling closes the backfill window: a late replacement re-checks settled? under the cycle lock and stands down
9 / 10
A short initial cycle still reaches review and release. The target never shrinks, the intake window closes around it
