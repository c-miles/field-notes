The Productivity
Dashboard

What we're building for auditor workload, and how

01 · Why

Admins can't see the team

  • No single view ranks auditors by completed output, or translates that output into hours
  • An Initial 2 review and a Monthly audit are not the same work, raw counts mislead
  • Open workload is invisible until something slips, overload shows up as overdue audits, after the fact
  • Rebalancing means knowing who's qualified and who has room, knowledge that lives in people's heads
  • Timeframes don't flex: today for a pulse, weeks and months for trends
“How productive is my team?”Every day
“Who is overloaded right now?”Every day

02 · What

One dashboard,
two tabs, one drawer

  • Team Productivity · completed audits per auditor, ranked, translated into productive hours
  • Current Workload · what's open per auditor, projected hours of backlog
  • Drill-down drawer · click any auditor, see their breakdown by audit type and client
  • In-context reassignment · move open work to a qualified auditor without leaving the screen

Scoped to

Kode-side admins.
Full auditor visibility.
Flexible timeframes,
defaulting to this week.

Per the brief: a lead compares people and rebalances work on one screen instead of stitching together reports.

05 · The values

Seeded from reality,
tuned by admins

  • Seeds come from measured history, the capped medians on the previous slide, not guesses
  • One field on the existing chart-kind admin form, adjustable any time
  • The seed task re-runs safely: it fills blanks only, never overwrites a hand-set value
  • New chart kinds start visibly “not set”, nothing ever silently prices at zero
  • Hours recalculate against current values, frozen history is a parked follow-up if product wants it

09 · Judgment calls

Four decisions, and why

Minutes live on chart kinds

One integer on the record that already exists, editable on the chart-kind admin form. The Platform sync only ever writes display_name, so a hand-set value can't be clobbered.

Live recalc, snapshot parked

Hours compute against current values. Freezing history at completion time is purely additive later, so it waits for a real product answer instead of shipping speculatively.

Capacity cut

Product dropped slot gating. Reassignment shows qualified candidates with their current load and trusts the lead's judgment, no capacity model to invent.

One minutes seam

Both query objects resolve expected minutes through a single shared helper. The precision upgrade later swaps that seam's interior and deletes nothing.

10 · The payoff

Reassignment,
in context

The drawer's reassign flow is assembled entirely from primitives that already exist and already power auto-assignment. Nothing novel to invent, just a new surface on trusted machinery.

1Expand Reassign on any line in an auditor's workload drawer
2AuditAccess.eligible_assignees_for lists only qualified candidates
3Each candidate shows their current load, the lead judges capacity by eye
4Audits::ManualAssigner executes, drawer and table refresh in place

11 · Then

Measured, not modeled

Platform already times every coding session, pickup to submit. After go-live, each audit's expected minutes come from what its actual chart took to code, times the accepted 1.5x rule, with the standard values as permanent fallback.

12 · Scope

Cut, parked,
or post-MVP

  • Capacity model and slot gating · cut by product, candidates show their current load instead
  • Frozen history for productive hours · parked on an open product question, hours recalculate live for now
  • Overdue indicators · shown in the mock, but no due-date data exists yet to power them
  • Measured per-audit times · not cut, sequenced: the Post Go-Live milestone on the previous slide
Everything cut or parked is additive later. Nothing here blocks go-live. Scope rule
